Rexburg, 2017 - The rain pitter-pattered across the window, dancing it's shadow along the walls of the dimly lit office. YOUR NAME sat opposite the designer, a cracked and splintered desk sat between them that had taken it's fair share of bullets over the course of a long and dangerous career. It's a hard road to stay on, but bad design is everywhere, sometimes in your own backyard. The client sat, fidgeting in their seat, like so many had done before. Same seat, same office, same problem. You see, sometimes, relationships just go stale. No amount of work will fix what is broken at it's core, and it just gets easier to make it go away. He'd been together with his website for six long years, but it was the designers job to make sure it never made it to a seventh. He had all the tools in his arsenal for any job, from video and web coding experience, to illustration and 3D modelling work. But when you stepped into that office, he knew you only had one question; How do you know he won't get caught?
